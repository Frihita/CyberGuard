Data Protection Tips

Learn more about protecting your personal information.

Apps should never ask for your passwords, OTPs, or master PINs. Be wary of apps asking for accessibility services or screen recording permissions unless absolutely necessary for their core function.
Fake screens often have poor grammar, blurry logos, or ask for permissions that don't match the app's purpose (e.g., a calculator asking for contact access).
Enable 2-Step Verification, perform regular Security Checkups in your Google settings, and review "Third-party apps with account access".

Fake Call Script Library

Learn to recognize common scam calls and how to respond.

What they say: "Your computer has a virus. Download this remote access tool so we can fix it."

What they really mean: They want to install malware and steal your passwords or lock your files for ransom.

What you should say: "I'll take it to a local certified professional. Goodbye."

What they say: "Unusual activity on your account. Verify your identity by telling me the OTP sent to your phone."

What they really mean: They are trying to log into your account and need the OTP to bypass security.

What you should say: "I'll call the bank back using the number on my official card."

What they say: "Congratulations! You've won a new iPhone / free vacation! To claim your prize, you just need to pay a small shipping fee."

What they really mean: There is no prize. They want to steal the 'fee' and your credit card details.

What you should say: "I never entered a contest. Hang up immediately."

What they say: "This is the IRS/Police. There is a warrant for your arrest unless you pay your fine in gift cards."

What they really mean: Government agencies never call to threaten arrest or ask for payment in gift cards/crypto.

What you should say: "Official agencies communicate via mail. Please send a notice."

What To Do If You're Hacked

Immediate steps to take if you suspect your accounts are compromised.

Immediate Steps:

  • Try to sign in and reset your password immediately. If you can, enable 2FA.
  • If you cannot sign in, use the account recovery option provided by the service.
  • Check for forwarding rules or automatic replies set by the hacker.
  • Log out from all devices.

Do This

  • Act immediately
  • Enable 2FA
  • Check connected apps

Don't Do This

  • Don't panic and delete account
  • Don't reply to hacker emails
  • Don't ignore it
Request a login link or security code from the platform. Use the 'Recover My Account' feature. Revoke access to suspicious third-party apps and change passwords for linked accounts.

Immediate Steps:

  • Use 'Find My Device' (Android) or 'Find My' (iPhone) to locate or erase.
  • Contact your carrier to suspend service and block the SIM card.
  • Change passwords for critical accounts (Email, Bank, Social).
  • Report theft to police for insurance claims.

Do This

  • Remotely lock/erase device
  • Contact carrier to block SIM
  • File police report

Don't Do This

  • Don't confront the thief
  • Don't wait to take action
  • Don't assume screen lock is enough
